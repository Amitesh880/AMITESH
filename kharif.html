Kharif Crops

Rice

Rice is the most important kharif crop in India. It thrives in hot and humid climates with plenty of rainfall. It's a staple food for a large part of the world's population.

Maize (Corn)

Maize is another major kharif crop. It requires a warm climate and is grown extensively in various regions. It's used as food, fodder, and for industrial purposes.

Cotton

Cotton is a significant cash crop that is grown during the kharif season. It requires a warm climate and a moderate amount of rainfall. Cotton is used primarily in the textile industry.

Soybean

Soybean is an important kharif legume crop. It is grown for its high-protein seeds, which are used to produce various food products, oil, and animal feed. Soybeans are also a key source of biofuel production, contributing to renewable energy resources.
