Timing and Method of Application

Basal Application

Basal application involves applying fertilizers at the time of planting or sowing. This method ensures that nutrients are available to the plants right from the beginning of their growth cycle.

Top Dressing

Top dressing is the application of fertilizers to the soil surface after the crops have been planted. It is typically done to provide additional nutrients during the growing season.

Split Application

Split application involves dividing the total amount of fertilizer into multiple smaller doses applied at different stages of crop growth. This method helps in better nutrient management and reduces the risk of nutrient loss.

Foliar Application

Foliar application is the spraying of liquid fertilizers directly onto the leaves of the plants. This method allows for quick nutrient absorption and is often used to correct nutrient deficiencies.

Post Harvest

Post-harvest application involves adding fertilizers to the soil after the crops have been harvested. This helps in replenishing the soil nutrients and preparing it for the next planting season.

Broadcasting

Broadcasting is the even distribution of fertilizers over the entire field. This method is simple and quick but may result in uneven nutrient distribution if not done properly.

Band Placement

Band placement involves applying fertilizers in bands or strips near the seed or plant rows. This method ensures that nutrients are readily available to the plants and reduces nutrient loss.

Drilling

Drilling is the placement of fertilizers into the soil using a drill. This method ensures precise placement of nutrients at the desired depth, promoting better root development and nutrient uptake.

Side Dressing

Side dressing is the application of fertilizers along the sides of the plant rows during the growing season. This method provides additional nutrients to the plants when they need them the most.

Fertigation

Fertigation is the application of fertilizers through the irrigation system. This method allows for precise nutrient delivery and efficient water use, promoting better crop growth and yield.

Deep Placement

Deep placement involves placing fertilizers deep into the soil, close to the plant roots. This method reduces nutrient loss through leaching and ensures that nutrients are available to the plants for a longer period.
