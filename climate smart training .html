Climate Smart Training

How It Is Practiced

Climate smart training involves integrating climate considerations into agricultural practices to enhance resilience, reduce emissions, and increase productivity. This includes using climate-resilient crop varieties, optimizing water management, and adopting sustainable land management practices. Training programs focus on building capacity to understand and implement these practices effectively.

Where to Practice

  • FAO eLearning Academy: Offers courses on climate-smart agriculture practices and technologies.
  • Local Agricultural Extension Services: Provide workshops and hands-on training in climate-smart practices.
  • Community-Based Programs: Engage local farmers in learning and applying climate-smart techniques.
  • Online Platforms: Websites like Coursera and edX offer courses on climate-smart agriculture.
  • NGO Initiatives: Organizations like the World Bank and IFAD support climate-smart training programs globally.

Benefits

  • Increases agricultural productivity and food security.
  • Reduces greenhouse gas emissions from farming activities.
  • Enhances resilience to climate change impacts.
  • Promotes sustainable use of natural resources.
  • Improves livelihoods of farming communities.

On Which Crops

  • Maize: Drought-resistant and high-yield varieties.
  • Rice: Flood-tolerant and water-efficient varieties.
  • Wheat: Heat-tolerant and disease-resistant varieties.
  • Legumes: Beans, lentils, and peas with improved resilience.
  • Vegetables: Climate-resilient varieties of tomatoes, peppers, and leafy greens.

Other Essentials

  • Soil Health: Regular soil testing and organic amendments to maintain fertility.
  • Water Management: Efficient irrigation systems and rainwater harvesting.
  • Pest Management: Integrated pest management techniques to control pests sustainably.
  • Climate Data: Access to weather forecasts and climate projections for informed decision-making.
  • Community Engagement: Involving local communities in planning and implementing climate-smart practices.
