Practice Integrated Nutrient Management

Assess Soil and Crop Needs

Evaluate the specific nutrient requirements of your crops and the current condition of your soil. Conduct soil tests to identify nutrient deficiencies and understand the organic matter content.

Combine Nutrient Sources

Use a combination of organic and inorganic nutrient sources to meet crop needs. This includes compost, manure, biofertilizers, and chemical fertilizers.

Follow Recommended Practices

Adhere to best practices for nutrient application, including proper dosages, timing, and methods to ensure efficient nutrient use and minimize environmental impact.

Minimize Nutrient Loss

Implement strategies to reduce nutrient loss through leaching, runoff, and volatilization. Techniques include using cover crops, mulching, and controlled-release fertilizers.

Incorporate Crop Residues

Return crop residues to the soil to enhance organic matter content and nutrient cycling. This practice improves soil structure and fertility over time.

Rotate and Diversify Crops

Practice crop rotation and diversification to improve soil health, break pest and disease cycles, and optimize nutrient use efficiency.

Monitor and Evaluate

Regularly monitor soil and crop health to assess the effectiveness of nutrient management practices. Adjust nutrient applications based on soil test results and crop performance.

Benefits of INM

  • Improves soil health and fertility
  • Enhances crop yield and quality
  • Reduces environmental impact
  • Promotes sustainable agriculture
  • Increases resilience to climate change
